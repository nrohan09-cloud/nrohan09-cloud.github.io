Project Intern

I was part of the Spatial Navigators team and was supervised by Dr V Srinivasa Chakravarthy. Programmed a virtual animal to move randomly but clockwise in a triangular enclosure. The generated trajectory points and other information were preprocessed before being fed into a stacked autoencoder. The bottleneck of the second autoencoder when analyzed shows cell responses similar to ones recorded in actual experiments with rats while using VR to lead them to virtual rewards. Also adapted lines of code to model a virtual animal moving in an enclosure with 4 compartments. A similar procedure as before was followed with this trajectory and the responses were again similar to actual experimental results obtained from rats. Presented our research to the Bernstein Conference, a renowned conference worldwide in computational neuroscience. Also contributed to a related paper that is still in the process of being written.
